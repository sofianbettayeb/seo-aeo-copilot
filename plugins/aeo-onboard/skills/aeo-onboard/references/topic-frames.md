# Topic Frames — 5-Axis Taxonomy

Every brand gets one topic per axis. The five axes span the buyer journey from broad demand to bottom-of-funnel intent. If the brand genuinely doesn't fit an axis, substitute with a second outcome topic and document the substitution in the description.

---

## Axis 1: Broad Category

The umbrella term that captures the brand's primary use case. This is the topic a buyer types when they don't yet know any vendor names.

**Intent**: top-of-funnel, category-aware.
**Prompt count**: 5–6.
**Keywords**: the category term + 2–4 close synonyms.

**Examples**:
- cloudnc.com → "AI CAM Programming Automation"
- a SaaS contract review tool → "AI Contract Review Software"
- a logistics tracking platform → "Real-Time Freight Visibility Software"

---

## Axis 2: Primary Integration Ecosystem

The dominant tool, platform, or stack the brand integrates with. Captures buyers who search by their existing tooling rather than by category.

**Intent**: mid-funnel, stack-aligned.
**Prompt count**: 5–6.
**Keywords**: the integration name + the category term scoped to it.

**Examples**:
- cloudnc.com → "Fusion 360 CAM Add-ons"
- a CRM enrichment tool → "Salesforce Data Enrichment Apps"
- an Airtable extension → "Airtable Workflow Automation Tools"

---

## Axis 3: Secondary Integration Ecosystems

Adjacent stacks the brand also serves. Combine 2–4 ecosystems into one topic if they share intent (e.g., professional CAM users across Mastercam, NX, GibbsCAM, SolidCAM).

**Intent**: mid-funnel, switch- or complement-driven.
**Prompt count**: 5–6.
**Keywords**: the secondary platform names.

**Examples**:
- cloudnc.com → "Pro CAM Users — Mastercam, NX, GibbsCAM, SolidCAM"
- a CRM enrichment tool → "HubSpot, Pipedrive, Zoho Enrichment"
- a project management add-on → "Notion, ClickUp, Linear Automation"

---

## Axis 4: Industry Vertical

The highest-value sector(s) the brand serves. Captures buyers who search by industry compliance or use case.

**Intent**: mid- to bottom-funnel, vertical-bound.
**Prompt count**: 5–6.
**Keywords**: the vertical + the category term.

**Examples**:
- cloudnc.com → "Aerospace & Defense CNC Machining"
- a contract review tool → "Healthcare Contract Review Software"
- a logistics platform → "Cold-Chain Pharma Logistics Tracking"

---

## Axis 5: Outcome / Business Problem

The operational result buyers care about. Productivity, quoting, throughput, compliance, cost-per-unit, time-to-value. Bottom-of-funnel, problem-aware.

**Intent**: bottom-funnel, problem-led.
**Prompt count**: 5–6.
**Keywords**: outcome verbs + business unit.

**Examples**:
- cloudnc.com → "Machine Shop Productivity & Quoting"
- a contract review tool → "Legal Team Contract Throughput"
- a logistics platform → "Reducing Freight Dwell Time"

---

## Substitution rules

If the brand has no integration ecosystem (rare for B2B SaaS, common for B2C or standalone hardware):
- Replace Axis 2 with a **persona-driven topic** ("DTC Founders", "Independent Designers").
- Replace Axis 3 with a **second outcome topic** or a **use-case topic**.

Document any substitution in the topic description so the design choice is traceable when the user reviews the report.
